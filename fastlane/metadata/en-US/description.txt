Most pickleball players practice by guessing. A drill half-remembered from a video, no record of what was actually worked on, and no idea whether the weak areas are getting any attention. Kitchenline gives solo practice a structure.

The Drills tab holds 18 real, named drills across four categories: Dinking, Third Shot Drop, Serve and Return, and Footwork. Each drill opens on an animated top-down court diagram where a glowing ball traces every rep across the kitchen line. You run reps, mark each one In or Out, and follow real coaching cues. Every rep marked In locks its arc onto the Home tab's daily progress court, which accumulates arcs from every drill you practice that day and resets at midnight. On the Skills tab you rate your own game 1 to 5 in each category and pick the specific shots giving you trouble, like Third Shot Drop or Backhand Dink.

The full drill library, the animated court diagrams, the rep counter, and the skill tracker are all free. Kitchenline Pro, an auto-renewing monthly subscription at $4.99 per month, adds two things. The AI weekly plan on the Plan tab turns your self-rated weak shots and today's available practice minutes into a multi-day schedule of specific drills and rep counts. Ghost-rally mode animates a simplified opponent dot on the court diagram, showing where an opponent would move during the exact drill you are practicing. AI requests are processed via our servers, and no account is needed. If a request fails, the app quietly shows a sensible built-in plan instead.

No matchmaking, no feeds, no social layer. Just you, a court, and a plan.
